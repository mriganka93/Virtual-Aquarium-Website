Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are vibrant underwater ecosystems made up of colonies of tiny animals called corals. These reefs provide shelter and food for a diverse array of marine life, making them one of the most biologically rich environments on Earth. Coral reefs protect coastlines from erosion, support fishing industries, and are vital for maintaining ocean health. Their stunning colors and complex structures make them a true underwater wonder.

Kelp Forests

Kelp forests are underwater ecosystems dominated by large, fast-growing brown seaweeds called kelp. These forests create complex, three-dimensional habitats that provide shelter and food for a wide variety of marine animals, including fish, sea otters, and invertebrates. Kelp forests also help protect coastlines by reducing the impact of strong waves and play a crucial role in maintaining ocean biodiversity.

Mangroves

Coastal wetlands found in tropical and subtropical regions, mangrove forests have salt-tolerant trees that thrive in brackish water. They act as nurseries for many marine species and provide vital protection against erosion.

Estuaries

Estuaries are areas where freshwater from rivers meets and mixes with seawater. These habitats are rich in nutrients and support a wide variety of plant and animal species, including fish, shellfish, and migratory birds.
